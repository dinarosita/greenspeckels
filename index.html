Poinsettia

Euphorbia pulcherrima

The beginning:

Purchased Dec 2021 from Home Depot.

About:

Native to Mexico and Central America

Care:

  • Full to partial sun: sunny window.
  • Water when soil surface dry to touch. Saturate soil until water runs through the drainage holes, but do not let the plant sit in water. Do not overwater.
  • To retain blooming as long as possible, keep temperature at 18-24 °C.
  • Low humidity hurts.
  • Do not fertilize during the blooming season.

References:

  • It's tricky to make it rebloom in the next Christmas season. Here's instruction to follow:
    How to Grow and Care for Poinsettias Indoors

Fern

The beginning:

Purchased as seedlings between spring and fall 2021.

Care:

Humidity high, non-direct sunlight.

Problem and recovery:

Dried out late November. Maybe due to low humidity and direct window sunlight. Kept in bathroom to keep the humidity high, and away from direct light. Dried out foliages were pruned away but not all at once to prevent too much shock.

It takes a few weeks, but new growth are lushy and beautiful. On 18 Dec, the last original mature foliage was sacrificed. Plant put back on the desk but far from the window.

References:

  • The Spruce: How to Keep Your Ferns Thriving Indoors
  • West Coast Garden: How to Care for Your Indoor

Chili

The beginning:

Planted from seeds from 1 Thai bird eye chili from grocery store in spring or early summer 2021.

Care:

Fertilize every 2 week (next Christmas weekend)

A little story:

Planted from 1 chili pepper in spring or early summer 2021. Majority of the seeds grew into seedlings, there were so many. Unfortunately due to space limitation, only less than 10 is kept. Grow so fast during the peak of summer into a meter tall.

Hand polinated. First green chili was seen I think September 2021. They grow in size quite fast, but remain green for a long time. First observed red ripe chili was December.

Second Home generation intitiated on 22 December 2021 from one overripe red chili with soft brown tip.

Wild Flower

The beginning:

Rescued from the sidewalk late autumn 2021 when the temperature started to drop below 0 °C, together with 3 other types of weeds. They looked beautiful and weather didn't beat them up yet. Dandelion-like flowers were blooming and some turning into seed puffs. Brought home mainly for Olivia. It was the day she fell on her nose and forehead. She likes the soft seed puffs. Kept indoor.

The end:

After the seed puffs dispersed, the arrangement was mostly wilted. They are then sacrificed. RIP wild flowers.
