Green Speckles

Poinsettia

Euphorbia pulcherrima

Updates:

Dec 2021: Purchased from Home Depot.

Jan 2022: The red leaves tend to get black spot (rotting? dissease? wilting?) easily. While the green leaves are fine. Such leaves are removed regularly.

23 Jan: Still luscious and have a lot of red. The green leaves are still turning to red here and there, balancing the red removal due to black spotting.

31 Jan: Poinsettia becomes droopy very fast when the soil becoming to dry. But upon watering, it perks up very quickly. maybe even within 1-2 hours. We took photo of before and after watering today.

Early Feb: New leaves starting to grow from the middle crown where old flowers gone.

18 Feb: Budding! About the red leaves, even though many are removed as they get black spots, there's always ones turning red. So overall it's always looking red on top.

About:

Shrub native to Mexico and Central America. The flowers are yellow and tiny, the large red foliages are the floral bracts, which are modified leaves. Also called Mexican flameleaf.Typically forced to bloom for holiday season. Not toxic to people and pet.

Care:
  • Bright difused sunlight. Near sunny window with 6-8 hours of light a day. Direct sunlight can burn bracts and leaves.
  • Water when soil surface dry to touch. Saturate soil until water runs through the drainage holes, but do not let the plant sit in water. Do not overwater.
  • To retain blooming as long as possible, keep temperature at 18-24 °C.
  • Low humidity hurts.
  • Fertilizing:
    • All purpose fertilizer.
    • Not when blooming.
    • Fertilize a dose at late March / early April. Prune at this time too.
    • Continue once a month during spring and summer. half strength.
