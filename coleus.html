Green Speckles

Coleus

Plectranthus scutellarioides

About:

Tender tropical plant, native to Java. Perennial in warmer zone, annual otherwise.

Updates:
  • 30 Dec 2021: Seeds (McKenzie) planted in new soil (MiracleGro potting mix), with rocks at the bottom of the pot. Covered in paper towel to avoid fungus gnat investation.
  • 11 Jan 2022: Seedling started coming. They are up to 1 cm now.
  • 23 Jan : More seedlings are coming. leaves enlarginf. More thriving in general even though height wise it's still about 1-1.5 cm.
  • 31 Jan: Red coloring starts to show up in some leaves.
  • 7 Feb: Thinning the seedlings. Seems like majority of seeds are growing and the leaves are large. The container is crowded. Seedling are thinned, and regular thinning from now on will be necessary.
  • 9 Feb: Moved to southwest bedroom window behind tall bunny tail grass to provide shade from the sunlight.
  • 12 Feb: thinning.
  • 22 Feb: thinning continues. Red spots getting brighter and more prominent.
  • 12 Mar: Some of the green ones remain green, some start showing yellow center.
Care:
  • Part shade. For indoor containers, indirect sunlight in warm months, expose to filtered unlight during the winter. It does need some light even though just a little.
  • Hot and humid.
  • Water: keep moist but not soggy. Water every 2-3 days.
